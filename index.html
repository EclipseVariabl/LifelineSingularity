Quick Reference — Core Mechanics
Dice Pool

Roll a number of d6s equal to your relevant Stat + Skill rank. Each die showing 5 or 6 is a success.

Difficulty

1 success = Routine · 2 = Demanding · 3 = Severe · 4+ = Critical

Edge Dice

Roll extra dice from Trainings or circumstances. Only the highest result dice count when you have edge.

Strain

Checks without enough successes add Strain to a relevant system (Body, Mind, or Ship). 6 Strain = Condition.

Initiative

Roll Reflex + Awareness. Each success = 1 Initiative. Ties broken by Reflex score, then Awareness.

One ally may spend their action to Assist, granting +1d6 to any teammate's next roll this round.

Introduction

UCMO DOC-001

Lifeline Beyond is a tabletop roleplaying game about rescue, survival, and the relentless drive to save lives in the hostile depths of space. Players take the roles of Linebreakers — elite operatives of the Universal Crisis Management Organization (UCMO) — dispatched to some of the most dangerous locations in the known galaxy to extract, evacuate, and assist those in dire need.

This is not a game about military conquest or treasure hunting. It's a game about professionalism under pressure, teamwork in the face of catastrophe, and the very human (and not-so-human) cost of doing the most dangerous job in the universe.

The UCMO Oath
"No beacon goes unanswered. No life is expendable. We hold the line between the void and the living. We are the last signal. We are Linebreakers."

What You Need

  • Several six-sided dice (d6s) — 10 per player is comfortable
  • One Game Master (referred to as the Controller)
  • Two to five players as Linebreakers
  • Character sheets (use the builder in this handbook)
  • A mission scenario — or use the GM Station to generate one

The Setting

The game takes place in the Kevanis Expanse — a loose cluster of inhabited systems spanning 200 light-years, connected by a fractured network of jump relays called the Lattice. Dozens of factions, corporations, and planetary governments jostle for power. Among them, the UCMO operates as a neutral interstellar body — funded jointly, loyal to none, bound only to the mission.

The UCMO

Founded after the Hadaran Collapse — a catastrophic station failure that killed 800,000 colonists — the UCMO exists to ensure no disaster goes unanswered. It operates Response Cutters (small fast ships), Rescue Carriers (mid-range support vessels), and rare Leviathan-class Arks (massive evacuation platforms). Linebreakers crew these vessels and deploy into crisis zones.

Dice & Checks

Lifeline Beyond uses a dice pool system. When a Linebreaker attempts something uncertain and meaningful, they roll a pool of d6s. Each die showing a 5 or 6 is a success.

Building Your Pool

Your pool equals the relevant Core Stat (1–5) plus your Skill Rank (0–4). The Controller sets a Difficulty — a number of required successes.

Example
Commander Raxen (Physique 3, Athletics 2) attempts to pry open a damaged bulkhead. The Controller sets Difficulty 2 (Demanding). Raxen rolls 5 dice. Results: 2, 5, 3, 6, 1 — two successes. Just enough!

Difficulty Ratings

SuccessesDifficultyContext
1RoutineBasic task, minor risk
2DemandingTrained skill required, pressure exists
3SevereSignificant challenge, failure hurts
4CriticalNear-impossible, reserved for crises
5+LegendaryThe kind of rescue that becomes a story

Degrees of Success

Exceeding the required successes grants Surplus. Each surplus success can be spent for an extra benefit:

  • +1 die on your next related roll this scene
  • Perform the action faster (save one round)
  • Aid an ally without spending your next action
  • Gather additional information or reduce Strain by 1

Degrees of Failure

Failing a check doesn't always mean nothing happens — it means something goes wrong. The Controller chooses: Hard Failure (nothing and Strain), or Costly Success (you succeed but at a price — injury, time, resource loss).

Edge Dice (Advantage)

Certain Trainings, equipment, or circumstances grant Edge. When you have Edge, add extra dice to your pool (usually +1d6 to +3d6). If you have both Edge and Snag (disadvantage), cancel them one-for-one.

Snag (Disadvantage)

Adverse conditions give Snag. Remove dice from your pool before rolling, to a minimum of 1d6.

Assisted Rolls — The Lifeline Rule

One ally may Assist a Linebreaker. The assisting player describes how they help, rolls their own relevant Stat + Skill (Difficulty 1), and on success, adds +1d6 to the primary roller's pool. Only one assist per roll.

Group Checks

When the whole team must succeed together (navigating treacherous terrain, synchronized EVA), each character rolls. Count total successes across all rolls. Required total = Difficulty × number of characters. Half or more succeeding individually usually passes group morale checks.

Strain & Conditions

Linebreakers are resilient professionals, but the void takes its toll. Damage and stress are tracked as Strain across three tracks: Body, Mind, and (for ships) Hull.

Strain Tracks

TrackMax StrainSourceAt Max
Body6 + PhysiquePhysical damage, toxins, vacuumIncapacitated
Mind6 + ResolveHorror, trauma, psychic attackBreakdown
Hull6 + Ship ToughnessWeapon impacts, collisionsCatastrophic Failure

Taking Strain

When a character fails a dangerous roll, takes a hit, or is exposed to hazards, they take Strain equal to the attacker's roll or a fixed value. When a track fills:

  • Body maxed: Character is Incapacitated — cannot act, must receive First Aid (Diff 2) within 3 rounds or suffer a permanent Injury Condition.
  • Mind maxed: Character enters Breakdown — they act irrationally (Controller chooses behavior) until stabilized via Medic check or rest.

Conditions

Conditions are persistent negative states. Each imposes a Snag die or mechanical penalty. Characters can carry up to 3 Conditions; a 4th replaces the least severe and triggers a permanent consequence.

Recovery

Strain naturally recovers during Downtime. Body Strain clears at 3 per rest period with care, 1 without. Mind Strain clears at 2 per rest. First Aid (Medic check, Diff 2) restores 2 Body Strain immediately out of combat.

Skills represent specialized training. Each Skill is rated 0–4 (Untrained, Trained, Proficient, Expert, Master). At character creation, your Role grants starting skill ranks. You then spend 10 additional Skill Points, maximum rank 3 at creation.

Skills add directly to your dice pool. At rank 0, you can still attempt most checks — just using your Stat alone.

Species

Choose one of six sapient species at character creation. Each grants a stat modifier, a special ability, and a starting species trait.

Human (Terran)

"Adaptable beyond all reason." — Dr. Vael Sorrix, UCMO Xenology Division

  • Stat Bonus: +1 to any two different stats of your choice
  • Adaptable: Once per session, reroll any single die after seeing the result.
  • Jack of All Fields: Start with one additional Skill Point at rank 1 in any skill you don't already have.
  • Species Trait: Networked — Humans gain +1d6 when using the Assist action to help another character.

Vorrkai (Crustacean-Avian)

Towering cephalothorax-bodied beings from high-gravity tidally locked worlds. Their reinforced exoskeleton and compound vision make them exceptional in hostile environments.

  • Stat Bonus: +2 Physique, +1 Awareness, -1 Presence
  • Natural Armor: +1 to Defense Rating without wearing armor. Stacks with light armor only.
  • Compound Vision: Cannot be flanked. +1d6 to Awareness checks in low-light or cluttered environments.
  • Species Trait: Deep Breather — Vorrkai can hold their breath for 10 minutes and take half Strain from pressure changes.

Serelith (Symbiotic Cephalopod)

Bioluminescent cephalopod beings who communicate through skin-pattern and subsonic resonance. Their bond with their neural-symbiont grants extraordinary mental resilience.

  • Stat Bonus: +2 Intellect, +1 Resolve, -1 Physique
  • Neural Symbiont: Once per scene, reduce Mind Strain taken by 3.
  • Chromatic Communication: Can communicate silently with other Serelith at 30m. +1d6 to Deception and Empathy checks.
  • Species Trait: Eidetic Interface — Serelith may "upload" memories to compatible systems, bypassing Hacking difficulty by 1 once per session.

Keth'Aran (Silicate)

Crystalline silicon-based life forms from volcanic worlds. Their bodies are dense, slow-growing, and capable of incredible patience and structural endurance. They experience time differently.

  • Stat Bonus: +2 Endurance (as a Physique modifier), +1 Resolve, -1 Reflex
  • Igneous Constitution: Immune to heat and radiation Strain. Half Strain from toxic environments.
  • Stone Memory: Can perfectly recall any event witnessed. +2d6 to any knowledge or navigation check involving places visited.
  • Species Trait: Resonant Body — once per session, Keth'Aran can emit a shockwave (PHY + Endurance, Diff 2) that knocks all targets within 6m prone and deals 2 Body Strain.

Drellan (Synthetic-Organic Hybrid)

Genetically engineered and mechanically augmented beings originally created as laborers. They have won full sapient rights but bear the social and physical marks of their origins.

  • Stat Bonus: +1 to any three different stats (distributed as you choose)
  • Integrated Systems: Built-in HUD, comms, and diagnostic suite. +1d6 to Sensors and Systems checks.
  • Modular Frame: Can install one additional cybernetic enhancement beyond normal limits.
  • Species Trait: Emergency Reroute — when Incapacitated, make a Resolve check (Diff 2) to stay conscious for one more round at full function.

Aethyn (Gaseous Energy-Being)

Semi-corporeal beings of contained plasma and magnetic fields, native to gas giant atmospheres. They wear containment suits to interact with solid-matter civilization.

  • Stat Bonus: +2 Reflex, +1 Awareness, -1 Physique
  • Phase Step: Once per round, move through solid objects up to 1m thick. Cannot take objects with you.
  • EM Sense: Detect all powered electronic systems within 20m. +2d6 to Sensors against electronic targets.
  • Species Trait: Containment Hazard — if the Aethyn's suit is breached (3+ Body Strain in one hit), they must spend their action each round stabilizing or release an EM pulse (damages all electronics within 10m, Diff 2 Endurance for organics or 3 Body Strain).

Roles (Jobs)

A Linebreaker's Role defines their primary function aboard UCMO vessels and in the field. Each Role grants a skill package, a signature ability, a starting Training, and role-specific equipment access.

Field Medic

You hold the line between critical and stable. Where others see bodies, you see cases.

  • Skill Package: Medicine 3, Empathy 2, Endurance 1, Science 1
  • Signature: Triage Mastery — When stabilizing Incapacitated characters, you may heal 3 Body Strain instead of 2 with a successful Medicine check. On a Critical Success (3+ successes), also clear one Condition.
  • Starting Training: Field Surgeon
  • Equipment Access: Advanced Medkits, trauma tools, stims, nano-sutures

Combat Specialist

Your job is to control hostile environments — make them safer so the real work can happen.

  • Skill Package: Ranged Combat 3, Close Combat 2, Endurance 1, Intimidation 1
  • Signature: Suppressive Presence — Once per scene, declare a Suppression Zone (Diff 2 Command or Intimidation). Enemies in the zone take a Snag die on all offensive actions for 3 rounds.
  • Starting Training: Breach & Clear
  • Equipment Access: Military-grade weapons, tactical armor, breaching charges

Engineer

Everything that keeps people alive is a machine. You speak their language.

  • Skill Package: Engineering 3, Systems 2, Hacking 1, Science 1
  • Signature: MacGyver Protocol — Once per session, create a functional improvised tool or repair from available parts without needing specific components. Roll INT + Engineering (Diff = GM-set complexity).
  • Starting Training: Emergency Repair
  • Equipment Access: Engineering kits, plasma cutters, EMP devices, repair nanites

Pilot

You fly things that weren't meant to be flown in conditions they weren't designed for. That's Tuesday.

  • Skill Package: Piloting (Ship) 3, Piloting (Craft) 2, Navigation 2, Systems 1
  • Signature: Void Instinct — Reduce the Snag penalty from adverse flight conditions (debris fields, atmospheres, combat maneuvers) by 1d6. Once per scene, make an emergency maneuver without spending an action.
  • Starting Training: Precision Approach
  • Equipment Access: Enhanced flight suits, navigation rigs, sensor boosts

Negotiator

Half the obstacles between survivors and safety are human. You remove them.

  • Skill Package: Negotiation 3, Empathy 2, Insight 2, Deception 1
  • Signature: Read the Room — At the start of any social encounter, roll AWR + Insight (Diff 1). On success, the Controller reveals one hidden motivation or secret of a target NPC.
  • Starting Training: Silver Tongue
  • Equipment Access: Diplomatic credentials, translation matrix, signal jammers

Recon Scout

You go first. You find out what's there. You come back.

  • Skill Package: Stealth 3, Sensors 2, Survival 2, Athletics 1
  • Signature: Ghost Protocol — Once per scene, become undetectable to electronic systems (thermal, motion, EM) for up to 3 rounds. Movement halved.
  • Starting Training: Shadow Step
  • Equipment Access: Recon drones, cloaking patches, grapple lines, sensor arrays

EVA Specialist

Between the hull and the void, you work. Most people can't even look out there. You live there.

  • Skill Package: EVA Operations 3, Athletics 2, Engineering 1, Endurance 2
  • Signature: Void Walker — Take no Snag in zero-gravity or vacuum environments. Add +1d6 to all checks made in EVA suits.
  • Starting Training: Mag-Lock Mastery
  • Equipment Access: Advanced EVA suits, tether systems, micro-thrusters, hull-cutters

Commander

Someone has to make the call. That burden is yours. So is the glory when it works.

  • Skill Package: Command 3, Negotiation 1, Insight 2, Empathy 1, Navigation 1
  • Signature: Command Authority — Once per scene, issue a Command Order. All allies who follow it gain +1d6 on their next check this scene. Roll RES + Command (Diff 1) — failure means the order was unclear, no bonus.
  • Starting Training: Tactical Coordinator
  • Equipment Access: Tactical HUD, command uplink, priority comms channel

Sensor Operator

You see what others can't. The signals, the patterns, the life signs buried in the noise.

  • Skill Package: Sensors 3, Science 2, Hacking 1, Navigation 1
  • Signature: Lifesign Lock — Once per scene, perform a deep scan of an area. Identify the location, number, and rough condition of all living beings in a 500m radius. Roll INT + Sensors (Diff 2).
  • Starting Training: Deep Scan Protocol
  • Equipment Access: Enhanced sensor rig, bio-scanner, signal amplifier

Rescue Diver

Into the flooding compartment, the burning wing, the collapsing corridor — that's where you go.

  • Skill Package: Athletics 2, Endurance 2, Engineering 1, Medicine 1, EVA Operations 1, Survival 1
  • Signature: Extrication Expert — When extracting trapped or restrained beings, reduce Difficulty of all related checks by 1 (minimum 1). Can move a rescued survivor and still take a standard action.
  • Starting Training: Rapid Extraction
  • Equipment Access: Extrication kit, hydraulic rescue tools, quick-harness, breather masks

Hacker

The station's systems will obey you. The hostile ship's will too. Eventually.

  • Skill Package: Hacking 3, Systems 2, Science 1, Sensors 1
  • Signature: Zero-Day Arsenal — Maintain a cache of exploit tools. Once per session, automatically succeed on one Hacking check without rolling. On critical missions, this recharges at session start.
  • Starting Training: Ghost Signal
  • Equipment Access: Breach deck, signal splitter, neural tap, deep-core ICE breaker

Xenospecialist

When the crisis involves something nobody's seen before, you're the one they call.

  • Skill Package: Xenology 3, Science 2, Empathy 1, Survival 1
  • Signature: First Contact Protocol — When encountering unknown organisms or cultures, roll INT + Xenology (Diff 1). On success, gain one critical insight that grants +1d6 on all interactions or combat with that entity this scene.
  • Starting Training: Xenobiological Resilience
  • Equipment Access: Specimen containers, translation matrix, xenobio scanner, universal quarantine kit

Combat

Combat in Lifeline Beyond is tactical, dangerous, and should always feel like the last resort. Linebreakers aren't soldiers — they're rescuers who sometimes have to fight.

Combat Sequence

  1. Declare Surprise: If one side ambushes, they act first without an initiative roll.
  2. Roll Initiative: Roll REF + Awareness. Successes determine order (highest first, ties by REF).
  3. Take Turns: Each combatant takes one turn per round.
  4. End of Round: Resolve ongoing effects, reduce duration timers.

On Your Turn

Each turn, you may take one Standard Action, one Bonus Action, and one Move (up to 10m on foot).

Attacking

Roll your attack pool. Successes exceeding the target's Defense Rating become Hit Dice. Each Hit Die causes Strain equal to the weapon's damage. Armor reduces Strain taken (see Equipment).

Example
Kessel fires her pulse rifle (Damage 3) at a pirate with Defense 2. She rolls REF 3 + Ranged Combat 3 = 6 dice. Results: 2, 5, 6, 1, 5, 3 — 3 successes. That's 1 hit die (3 - 2 = 1). The pirate takes 3 Body Strain. Their armor (value 1) reduces it to 2.

Grappling

Roll PHY + Close Combat opposed by target's PHY + Athletics. On success, target is Restrained (Snag on all actions, cannot move). Maintain grapple as bonus action each turn.

Rescue Operations

At the heart of every mission is the rescue. These mechanics govern the core UCMO activities: extracting survivors, stabilizing crises, and keeping people alive.

The Clock

Most rescue missions run against a Rescue Clock — a countdown representing structural failure, oxygen depletion, fire spread, or other ticking threats. The Clock has 6 segments. Each failed meaningful check costs 1 segment. When the Clock hits 0, catastrophic failure occurs (survivors die, structure collapses, etc.).

Mass Evacuation

When evacuating groups of survivors, treat each 10 survivors as one unit. Each unit requires a successful Coordination Check (Command or Piloting, Diff 2). Failure costs Rescue Clock segments and may separate units.

Rescue environments deteriorate. Each scene change or failed clock check prompts a Hazard Roll (see GM Station). Linebreakers must adapt their tactics as conditions worsen.

Ship Combat

Ship combat is crew-coordinated. Each crew station handles one aspect per round. The Pilot drives initiative and evasion; Gunners attack; Engineers manage systems; Sensor Op provides intelligence.

Ship Combat Sequence

  1. Both pilots roll Piloting (Ship) + Speed. Higher result goes first.
  2. Active ship takes one Helm Action and each station takes one Station Action.
  3. Passive ship responds.
  4. End of round: resolve boarding, damage, fires.

Helm Actions

  • Evasive Maneuvers: Roll Piloting + REF (Diff 2). Success grants +2 Defense this round.
  • Attack Run: Roll Piloting + REF (Diff 2). Success grants +1d6 to all weapons this round.
  • Break Away: Roll Piloting + Speed (Diff 3). Success disengages from combat range.
  • Ram: Roll Piloting (Diff 3). Both ships take 6 Hull Strain.
  • Rescue Approach: Navigate into docking range of a target despite hostile conditions (Diff 2–4).

Running the UCMO

The Controller runs the world around the Linebreakers. Your job isn't to defeat them — it's to put them in impossible situations and let them surprise you with solutions you didn't anticipate.

The Controller's Principles

  • The Clock is always ticking. Rescue missions have urgency. Never let a scene feel comfortable for too long.
  • Every failure costs something real. Failed checks should hurt — but not always in the ways expected. A missed Medicine roll might mean a survivor loses a limb, not that they die.
  • The UCMO is complicated. Bureaucracy, politics, budget cuts, and rival factions create friction even inside the organization.
  • Survivors are characters, not cargo. Give them names, fears, and motivations. It makes the rescue matter.
  • Reward clever problem-solving generously. This is a game about experts. Let them be competent.

Mission Structure

Standard Mission Phases

  1. Briefing: UCMO dispatch relays distress signal. Linebreakers receive mission parameters and Intel (partial, never complete).
  2. Transit: Travel to the scene. Opportunity for character development, preparation, and complications en route.
  3. Assessment: Arrive on scene. First look at the disaster. Initial checks reveal scope and immediate threats.
  4. Intervention: The rescue itself. Multiple overlapping objectives and crises.
  5. Extraction: Get everyone out. The Clock is usually at its most critical here.
  6. Debrief: Back at UCMO. Survivors are accounted for. What was learned? What went wrong? Advancement.

Threats & Antagonists

Minion-Grade Threats

Pirates, security drones, panicked mobs. Use simplified stat blocks — PHY/REF/INT each 1–3, 4 Body Strain, one relevant skill at rank 1–2. They activate in groups, sharing an initiative slot.

Standard Threats

Rival salvagers, aggressive security, corrupted officials. Full stats but at the lower range. 6–8 Body Strain, 2–3 skills at rank 2.

Elite Threats

Faction enforcers, rogue Linebreakers, dangerous specialists. Full stats at high range. One signature ability. 10+ Body Strain.

Crisis-Scale Threats

Natural disasters, out-of-control station AI, apex predators, Lattice storms. These aren't fought — they're survived and mitigated. Track their Escalation instead of HP (1–6 scale; at 6, catastrophic outcome).
